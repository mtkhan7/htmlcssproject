Unit 1: Improving productivity with IT

1.1 Purpose of Information Technology in modern business

Inofrmation Technology has a huge purpose and value in modern businesses. In recent years the purpose of I.T in modern businesses have played a big role in companies being more productive by using state of the art software and hardware in automating the process required by businesses. I.T has enabled big companies such as Amazon, Microsoft, Google to solve complex problems at a quicker pace. There is benefit for businesses when using I.T as companies are able to use improved hardware such as more memory, faster processor and more storage. This allows them to be able to complete multiple tasks at a quicker rate which would allow them to be cost efficient and make more sales which will lead to more profit for the business.

Implementing the use of Information technology in businesses have also allowed them to make better decisions by doing market research online or offline and efficiently obtaining data with utilizing applications like Microsoft CRM Dynamics, Google Analytics [2] This will enable the business to be able to identify what their customers needs and wants are and how they can supply them with products fitting their demand. By doing this the business would be able to provide products which customers prefer.

Information technology has also resulted in improving customer support by utilising multiple channels such as smartphones, emails, social media platforms. There are also cloud technologies which are easily available today and have helped many businesses be able to manage their resources in an efficient manner, cut costs and have allowed their employees to use any devices anywhere in the world to access their enterprise software[2].By doing this businesses are able to be well organised and have a systematic structure allowing them to be more cost efficient. Due to high competition in the business sector, being cost efficient will give businesses the upper hand and it is evident by companies such as Apple and Samsung, having advanced I.T features allows them to take control of the market and therefore having huge market share in smartphones.

1.2 methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

Since digital projects require a lot of time and attention there are numerous skills and tools which are essential to complete these tasks successfully. There should be a detailed plan for implementation of the idea[4], this is essential as you are able to plan in manageable steps. Planning is crucial as a digital project would require an individual to face multiple scenarios and a plan allows one to be time efficient.

A digital project being produced in regards to a client, it is essential to understand the requirements, time constraints and costs and limitations of the project. Clients have demands which have to be met in order for the project to be successful. Therefore being aware of the requirements and understanding how to accomplish them is an essential skill to complete a project.

Digital projects have a number of different forms, such as a web application or a desktop app. It is crucial to be aware of what technologies or development stack to use specifically for the product and whether there needs to be a collaboration with user design and development teams. Alongside being aware of this, having interpersonal and teamwork skills to deal with clients or your team is also an essential skill in order to achieve a successful digital project.

This would therefore require good knowledge of communication tools such as slack, flock, google hangout that allow messaging, file and code sharing between teams.  Having knowledge of tools such as Trello, Asana, Monday for project management in conjunction with the use of agile software development methodologies such as Kanban and Scrum[5] are must have in order to achieve a successful project.

These tools aid in the planning,development, testing and the deployment of the end product . Other tools such as draw.io and lucidchart allow the creation of diagrams and flowcharts for the product. This would once again be time efficient and allow the needs of the client to be met efficiently.

Another essential skill required is to be able to use programming and scripting languages such as Python Javascript C++ for coding and developing the product. After developing the product, you must test the product and in order to do this, one must require knowledge of different tools and skills such as reviewing peer code, bug testing, usability testing and use of testing applications[1]. Applying these tools and skills would allow a project to be created efficiently and therefore being successful in meeting its end goal.

1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail?

There are numerous reasons as to why digital projects fail. One of the main reason is due to the fact that projects do not have a structural process. This could be due to lack of research and not brainstorming the project in the planning phase.

This can lead to multiple complications causing the project to be unsuccessful. Whilst developing a digital project, a company has to take various factors into account for it to be successful. For example, teamwork, time constraints and process planning are very important. If there is no process, it will be difficult to deliver the project on time and for it to be cost effective leading to failure of the project. Therefore, It is imperative to have a structure and to divide the project between the team into small manageable tasks. This will allow the project to be developed efficiently and on time.

Other issues causing a digital project to fail involves poor communication between team members, lack of delegating tasks and not reviewing and testing the end product. If companies do not test their products they will not be able to find faults which can lead to vulnerabilities such as bugs, mistakes, and the failure of the product. Another factor could be due to lack of marketing and sales promotion alongside consumer feedback. This is vital for any digital project as consumer feedback allows room for improvements to be implemented. All the above elements if not taken into consideration will cause a digital project to fail.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

There are many legal guidelines and constraints that can impact digital projects. One of the most important guidelines can be considered is General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). This is known as a European regulation that came into effect on the 25th of May 2018 and is recognised across the entire EU region. The effect of this legislation allows individuals to have a sense of control over their data in regards to companies exploiting them.

The passing of this legislation has had a huge impact on digital projects as companies need to take into account data privacy and security standards when creating digital projects. Many online services now require consent when asking for data, whether the user is willing to provide the website with data e.g email, name and address. The regulation also prevents companies from collecting and storing data on the basis that they may need it in the future therefore showing that GDPR makes it easier for individuals to withdraw consent for their data. When creating digital projects this will have an impact as these are standards that need to be taken into account therefore one should map companies data and determine what data is required and should be kept in any online service being provided.

The GDPR also encourages organisations to improve cybersecurity and to keep on developing their measures in order to decrease the risk of any data breach. This will have an impact on a digital project, as you need to take into account the consequences that will occur if someone's data gets breached therefore good security measures need to be implemented to prevent this.
